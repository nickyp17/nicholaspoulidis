## Design Context

### Users
Recruiters reviewing the portfolio to assess technical skills, experience depth, and professional presence. They scan quickly — the interface needs to make a confident first impression and let them move through content without friction.

### Brand Personality
Precise, warm, dry-humored. Coffee-shop meets engineering notebook. The mocha palette and JetBrains Mono font are deliberate brand choices. Projects have coffee-themed names (Brewer, Grind, Roast, Drip) — there's quiet personality here, not just a résumé.

### Aesthetic Direction
Restrained editorial feel, inspired by martinsit.ca: centered, focused, minimal. Warm earth tones (cream, latte, mocha, espresso). JetBrains Mono with strong weight contrast for hierarchy. Light mode default; dark mode uses deep espresso tones — warm and intentional, not cold blue-black. Subtle dot grid background adds texture without noise. Sharp corners over rounded — precision is a value.

Anti-references: gradient text, glassmorphism, neon/cyan accents, generic rounded-card grids, hero metrics layouts, dark mode with glowing accents.

### Design Principles
1. **Warmth over flash** — color and tone create comfort; no gradients, glows, or theatrics
2. **Precision in detail** — sharp corners, strong type hierarchy, intentional spacing over uniform padding
3. **Let content lead** — no decorative noise; every element earns its place
4. **Light and dark as equals** — dark mode is warm espresso, not cold; both modes feel fully intentional
5. **Recruiter-readable** — name, role, experience, and contact are immediately scannable on first glance
